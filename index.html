Have a question? Check out the FAQ

What are Google Analytics segments?

Segments in Google Analytics are subsets of data that allow you to focus on specific groups of website visitors, such as those from a certain location, using a specific device, or completing a particular action

How can I create a segment in Google Analytics?

To create a segment in Google Analytics, go to the "Admin" section, select the "Segments" option under the "View" column, and click on "New Segment." You can then define the segment based on various criteria like demographics, traffic sources, and behaviors.

Why should I use segments in Google Analytics?

Segments allow you to isolate and analyze specific groups of users, helping you gain more granular insights into your website's performance.

How do I analyze segment performance in Google Analytics?

Once you apply a segment, you can view detailed reports on metrics like page views, bounce rate, conversion rate, and more, specifically for the selected segment. Compare different segments to see how they perform relative to each other and identify areas for improvement.

Can I use multiple segments in a single report?

Yes, Google Analytics allows you to compare up to four segments in a single report. This makes it easier to compare the behavior of different user groups side by side and gain more comprehensive insights.
